What We Do

Safety data, made simple.

Most school safety information is scattered across district pages, policy documents, and public records. This platform turns that into a clear, trusted, standardized view.

Transparent Safety Scorecards

See how Connecticut high schools compare on prevention, building security, classroom protection, emergency response, and transparency — all in one place.

Parent-Friendly Ratings

We turn hard-to-find safety information into a clear, understandable rating families can actually use — no policy documents or technical expertise required.

Built for Action

Help schools identify gaps and improve systems like panic alerts, anonymous reporting, and drill readiness — with actionable recommendations tied to each category.

How It Works

Simple for families.
Rigorous behind the scenes.

01
Research

We research public safety policies, district materials, and official school information using primary sources and government documents.

02
Verify

We verify key safety features through direct outreach, documentation review, and site-based evidence when available.

03
Publish

We score each school using a consistent methodology and publish easy-to-read results that families can understand and act on.

Designed for Credibility

What makes this model strong

Clear methodology schools can review

Every score is traceable back to a documented source and a defined criterion — no black boxes.

Evidence-based scoring

Ratings are grounded in documented safety features, not vague opinions or self-reported data from schools.

Actionable recommendations

Each category score comes with specific recommendations, helping schools understand exactly how to improve.

Connecticut pilot, then national expansion

Designed to scale from a Fairfield County pilot to a national school safety platform over time.
